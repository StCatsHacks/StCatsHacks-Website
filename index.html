StCatsHacks 2025

First Greek inter-school hackathon (with food & great prizes).

8th edition




  • Learn more

StCatsHacks 2025: "Aristotle’s Lab of Human Flourishing – AI for the Global Goals" StCatsHacks is the first inter-school Hackathon held in Athens, Greece. High-school and primary school students passionate about STEM will gather at St. Catherine’s British School for a two-day programming challenge. In teams of up to 5, with access to APIs and the guidance of dedicated mentors, students will design and develop innovative computer programs, evaluated on their creativity, functionality, and real-world impact. Now in its 8th edition, the theme is inspired by Aristotle’s concept of eudaimonia (human flourishing)—the pursuit of knowledge and virtue in service of the common good. This vision resonates strongly with the United Nations’ 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which call for collective action to build a just, sustainable, and thriving world. This year’s challenge is: "AI for Human Flourishing: Innovating for the 17 Global Goals." Participants are encouraged to harness the power of artificial intelligence to create solutions addressing one or more of the UN SDGs—ranging from eliminating hunger and poverty, advancing quality education and healthcare, promoting gender equality and climate action, to fostering peace, justice, and strong institutions. Just as Aristotle believed that knowledge should serve humanity, StCatsHacks invites students to transform ethical ideas into practical innovation, using technology to shape a future where both people and planet can thrive.

The competition categories are the following:
  • Minecraft World Building (Age 8-12)
  • Mindstorms EV3 Sumo (Age 8-13)
  • Robot Competition Line Racing (Age 13+)
  • Hack the box - Cybersecurity Competition Sponsored by Hackcraft and Hack The Box!
  • Open Competition - Aristotle’s Lab of Human Flourishing (All ages)
  • Slam - Game Competition Individual

{ FAQ }

What is a hackathon?

Two days to build your dream project, hardware or software.

When and where is it?

November 29th and November 30th @ St. Catherine's British School, Upper School.

What time does it start and end?

The event will begin at 12:00 on Saturday and will end at 6:00pm on Sunday.

What should I bring?

Your laptop, your resume, and some friends.

Who can participate?

High-school and college students, especially those interested in the field of STEM.

What if this is my first hackathon?

That's fine, don't worry; there is always a first time. We'll have mentors that will help you out.

What if I don't have an idea or a team?

That's absolutely fine! We'll help you find a team and an idea.
